Break the scrolling loop

You open Instagram for "just a minute." A while later you switch to YouTube, then TikTok. Daily limits are already spent by lunch, and most are too easy to tap past in the moment.

Scrollless works differently. Before you open any app you have picked, it asks: "How long do you want this session to be?" When the time is up, all of those apps lock at once. No switching to another one. No tapping past it. You take a short break, then you decide again.

How It Works

Why Scrollless?

Most screen time apps fight you. Scrollless puts you in charge.

You set the time

You choose how long each session lasts, like making a small promise to yourself. No surprise lockouts and no daily cap you forgot you set.

All apps lock together

Close Instagram, open YouTube, switch to TikTok. Sound familiar? Scrollless locks all the apps you picked at the same time, so there is nowhere to bounce to.

Short sessions are easier

Pick a short timer and you are in right away. Pick a long one and there is a brief wait first. A small nudge toward the shorter choice.

A short break in between

After each session there is a short break before you can go again. Long enough to interrupt the habit, short enough that it does not annoy you.

Based on how habits work

The design borrows from research on habits and on commitment devices, the trick of deciding now so the future you sticks to it. It works with your psychology, not against it.

Free to use

Scrollless is free, with a few light ads to keep it running. Prefer none? Remove them with a one-time purchase.

The story behind Scrollless

I built Scrollless because nothing else worked for me. The built-in tools were too easy to ignore, other apps were too rigid with daily limits, and the good ones put the useful parts behind a paywall. So I made my own while studying at HSG, and kept it free for everyone.
